How do you see the Philippine foreign minister going from cursing China to apologizing to China and praising Chinese foreign minister? What is the reason behind it?

Posted on 2021-05-06

Q:

How do you see the Philippine foreign minister going from cursing China to apologizing to China and praising Chinese foreign minister? What is the reason behind it?

Background:

In recent days, the Philippine Foreign Minister Lurchin has been dazzled by a series of enigmatic actions on China-related issues.
From “foul-mouthed” to China on social media, which drew criticism from the Philippine president, to a “180-degree turn” in attitude, apologizing to China and praising the Chinese foreign minister ……
What is the reason behind the move of “letting yourself go”?

A:

Dumb and Dumber Salary Fire:

It is mainly a dispute over the South China Sea.

The Philippine foreign minister’s foul mouth stems from the schizophrenia of the Duterte administration. On the one hand, the Duterte administration is dissatisfied with the Philippines’ current situation as a semi-colony of the United States, and the problems of drug proliferation in the country, and needs China’s support, so it has to adopt a relatively friendly attitude toward China.

However, on the other hand, the Duterte administration is gaining voter support with populism, and the nationalism behind the populism means that it is difficult for Duterte to adopt a more flexible attitude on the South China Sea dispute. To some extent, their concessions to China on the South China Sea issue will be questioned by voters for “giving up sovereignty”. And if he “gives up sovereignty” to China, then does the Philippines really want to be incorporated as a province of China? Is that different from the former president’s pro-U.S. role as a pawn of the U.S.?

This contradiction has always been present in the Duterte administration.

China is the best external assistance to change poverty and eliminate crime. But China and the Philippines are not without conflicting interests in terms of national positions, and if the latter is abandoned for the former, it would be hypocritical for Duterte to criticize the position of previous presidents as pawns of the United States on the South China Sea.

So it’s not hard to see that the Duterte administration is acting in what appears to be a schizophrenic manner.

By the way.

It’s amazing that Chinese diplomats are called “war wolves” for their reasoning, but no one calls the Philippine foreign minister a “war wolf” for his foul-mouthed outburst.

Miss Tassel:

When the Philippines made the disgusting South China Sea arbitration, China and the United States in the South China Sea once saber-rattling, the momentum of a showdown.

After the U.S. carrier battle group entered combat positions, China’s three fleets put up a shuttle posture. Soon after, the U.S. opened high and withdrew quietly.

Then in September, the new Philippine President Duterte began cursing the United States of America and calling the U.S. president a sn of b**h.

In October 2016, Duterte made a high-profile visit to China and delivered an interesting speech at the Great Hall of the People.

I myself have Chinese blood, Moro blood, and Unshiah blood in me. I have studied the traits of these peoples and it turns out that we Orientals are very culturally rich people. But the problem is that when you talk to Westerners, like Americans or other Westerners, you will find them very uncultured.

It will be the norm for a long time for the Philippines to swing between China and the U.S. It’s just that this swing will gradually tilt towards us, after all, we are right next to the Philippines, looking at them with a compassionate smile on our face.
