How do you rate "You at the Same Table", a Chinese cartoonist's depiction of China's support for India's fight against the epidemic?

Posted on 2021-05-04

Q:

How do you rate “You at the Same Table”, a Chinese cartoonist’s depiction of China’s support for India’s fight against the epidemic?

Background:

On the afternoon of May 2, “Wang Xiaowei, who loves to draw” released a cartoon titled “You at the Table”, depicting a panda supplying oxygen to an elephant who is seriously ill at the same table.
The creator of this work is a member of Shandong Cartoonists Association and an honorary member of Zhejiang Cartoonists Association. He told the Observer, “A distant relative is better than a close neighbor, and what China has shown in supporting India in the matter of fighting the epidemic is exactly the demeanor of a great nation, despite the fact that the two sides have had bad blood in the past.”

A:

Hi, I am Wang Xiaowei

I like to draw but don’t like to talk, but my editor teacher insisted me to talk, not to say no food.

The original intention of creation: from the news to learn about the epidemic in India, the people’s encounter is heartbreaking. But in the face of China’s hot help next door neighbor’s response is a big shock. We live on the same planet, and we are all a community of destiny. China and India are neighbors in Asia, so they should be in the same boat to help each other in the epidemic.

A distant relative is better than a close neighbor, so put aside your face and feel free to take in oxygen.

There are several key words that I do not interpret for discussion.

1、Sick image Pretentious posturing

2、The direction of the “38th parallel”

3、Oxygen intake nose under the cover of the arm

4、Needle stick doll

5、A glass of drink

6、Table
